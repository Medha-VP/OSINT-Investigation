# **<u>Advanced Search Techniques</u>** 

## **1. Aim** 

To compare the information obtained from different search engines and OSINT tools and understand how each platform contributes to an Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT) investigation. 

## **2. Objective** 

The objective of this practical is to investigate IBM using multiple search engines and compare the information returned by each. Since different search engines use different indexing methods, they may reveal unique publicly available information that others do not. This comparison helps demonstrate the importance of using multiple sources during an OSINT investigation. 

## **4. Target Selected** 

**Organization:** IBM (International Business Machines Corporation) 

**Official Website:** <u>https://www.ibm.com</u> 

## **5. Search Queries Used** 

## **Google** 

- site:ibm.com filetype:pdf 

- site:ibm.com intitle:login 

- site:ibm.com security 

## **Bing** 

- site:ibm.com filetype:pdf 

- site:ibm.com login 

- IBM security 

## **DuckDuckGo** 

- site:ibm.com filetype:pdf 

- site:ibm.com login 

- IBM security 

## **6. Findings** 

## **Google Search** 

Google returned a wide range of official IBM resources, including annual reports, whitepapers, technical documentation, product manuals, security-related pages, and login portals. The advanced search operators made it easy to locate publicly available documents and official web pages. 

## **Strengths** 

- Excellent for official documents and reports 

- Accurate search results 

- Strong support for advanced search operators 

## **Bing Search** 

Bing produced results similar to Google but also indexed some additional pages that were ranked differently. It surfaced technical documentation, career-related pages, and a few archived or less frequently accessed resources that were not immediately visible in Google's top results. 

## **Strengths** 

- Useful for discovering additional indexed pages 

- Good source for technical documentation 

- Different ranking can reveal overlooked resources 

## **DuckDuckGo Search** 

DuckDuckGo returned many of the same official IBM pages as Google and Bing while also presenting some results in a different order. It highlighted official documentation, securityrelated pages, support resources, and technical articles. The search engine was useful for 

validating findings and identifying publicly available information that may not appear prominently in other search engines. 

## **Strengths** 

- Provides alternative search results and ranking 

- Useful for verifying findings from other search engines

## **8. Observations** 

- Google provides the most comprehensive collection of official IBM web pages, research papers, technical documentation, and downloadable PDF files. 

- Bing indexes many of the same pages but also reveals additional or differently ranked content, making it useful for discovering resources that Google may not prioritize. 

- DuckDuckGo returns similar public information but uses a different indexing and ranking approach, which can surface useful resources that may not appear prominently in Google or Bing. 

- Using multiple search engines and OSINT tools provides a broader and more complete understanding of the target organization's public digital footprint. 

## **9. Key Takeaways** 

- No single search engine indexes all publicly available information. 

- Google is best suited for locating official documents, reports, and web pages. 

- Bing can reveal additional indexed resources and alternative search results. 

- DuckDuckGo helps validate findings and may surface useful pages through its alternative search indexing. 

- Combining multiple search engines improves the effectiveness and completeness of OSINT investigations. 

## **10. Conclusion** 

This practical demonstrated that relying on a single search engine does not provide a complete view of an organization's publicly available information. Google was highly effective for locating official documents, reports, and technical resources, while Bing provided additional indexed pages and alternative search results. DuckDuckGo complemented these findings by presenting similar information through a different search index and ranking algorithm, helping identify additional publicly available resources. By combining the strengths of these search engines, investigators can perform more comprehensive and accurate OSINT investigations using only publicly available information.
