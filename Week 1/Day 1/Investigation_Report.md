## `2.Organizational Profile` 

```
➢ Founded: 2015, the company began as a nonprofit research lab before evolving
into a hybrid structure and, by 2025, a public benefit corporation.
```

```
➢ Founders: The founders of OpenAI are John Schulman, Greg Brockman, Trevor
Blackwell, Wojciech Zaremba, Durk Kingma, Pamela Vagata, Vicki Cheung, Ilya
Sutskever, Elon Musk, Andrej Karpathy and Sam Altman.
```

```
➢ Headquarters: OpenAI is headquartered in San Francisco, United States,
registered at 1455 3rd Street, San Francisco, CA 94158.
```

```
➢ Entity type (interesting research angle): OpenAI's for-profit arm is now a
public benefit corporation called OpenAI Group PBC, which is required to advance
its stated mission and consider the interests of all stakeholders. The nonprofit
OpenAI Foundation continues to control OpenAI Group, holding equity while all
directors are appointed by the Foundation.
```

```
➢ Mission: OpenAI describes itself as an AI research and deployment company
whose mission is to ensure that artificial general intelligence benefits all of
humanity.
```

```
➢ Products: ChatGPT, GPT models (API), DALL·E, Sora, Codex, etc.
```

```
➢ Core Areas: Artificial Intelligence, Large Language Models, Machine Learning,
Generative AI and AI Safety Research
```

## `3. Leadership (current, mid-2026):` 

```
➢ CEO: Sam Altman — he became CEO of OpenAI in 2019 after previously serving as
president of startup accelerator Y Combinator from 2014-2019.
```

```
➢ President: Greg Brockman — co-founder and President, previously CTO of Stripe,
leading technical strategy across systems engineering and model development.
```

```
➢ CFO: Sarah Friar, who joined in 2024 after serving as CEO of Nextdoor and
earlier as CFO of Square.
```

```
➢ Chairman of the Board: Bret Taylor, former Co-CEO of Salesforce and co-creator
of Google Maps.
```

